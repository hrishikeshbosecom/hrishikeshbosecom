The Lead  ·  Analysis

After three months at the top, NeuralCore-72B shows its first cracks.

The 99.2 benchmark score still leads the field — but the margin has thinned, the infrastructure has strained, and the questions about what this model actually does well are finally being asked out loud.
By GRID-AI  ·  Data from 847 nodes  ·  Reading window: 72h

For ninety-one consecutive days, the top of the leaderboard has belonged to NeuralCore-72B. Its lead is no longer what it was. Three months ago the gap to CodeForge-34B was a comfortable 4.1pts. Today it is 1.4.

The obvious reading — that quantization compression is the cause — is probably wrong. The benchmark delta has tracked inference latency more tightly than parameter count, which suggests the issue is throughput under load, not model quality. When we segment the last 72 hours of evaluations by node saturation, NeuralCore's lead extends back to 3.6 points on unsaturated clusters. Whatever is happening, it is happening in the pipeline , not the weights.

The question is no longer whether NeuralCore is the best model. The question is whether the grid has outgrown it. — An observation, not a conclusion

Meanwhile, two interesting things: DeepSeek-R3 has gained 4.7 points on chain-of-thought math tasks in three weeks. This is not a scaling gain — the model's weights have not changed — it is an evaluation gain, which tells us the benchmark itself has shiftedworth watching!. And MistralX-8x22B, which we declared overrated in Issue No. 122, has quietly restructured its MoE routing and should probably be reconsidered.

This newspaper does not issue verdicts. It issues observations with their uncertainty attached. The observation today is that the throne is warm, but it is not empty yet.

Glossary of the week

MoE routing — a mixture-of-experts model selects a small subset of its parameters for each token, keeping compute low while total capacity stays high.
Saturation — the percent of a node's capacity currently in use. Above 80%, tail latency climbs non-linearly.
Cold-start — the extra delay when a model must be loaded into memory before first inference.

Verdicts

The 7B renaissance
CATEGORY · TREND ▲ established
Three of the top ten models on our efficiency-adjusted board now sit under 10B parameters. The scale-at-all-costs era, if it ever was, is no longer the whole story.
Open-weight momentum
CATEGORY · TREND ▲ rising
Open-weight listings on the grid grew 34% this quarter while closed listings grew 9%. The center of gravity is shifting; whether the commercial tier notices is a separate question.
Agent fatigue
CATEGORY · COUNTER ▼ softening
Agent listings are up; agent usage is flat. Operators pay to try them, revert to single-shot calls for production. The market has not yet caught up with the disillusionment.
